Kerela Floods



Severe floods affected the south Indian state Kerala, due to unusually high rainfall during the monsoon season. It was the worst flood in Kerala in nearly a century. Over 483 people died, and 140 are missing. It is the worst flood in Kerala after the great flood of 99 that took place in 1924.



Bengal Earthquake


4.8 Magnitude Earthquake Hits West Bengal's Bankura District.The tremors were felt in the district at around 10:39 am, it added. The epicentre of the tremor was 10 km below the surface of Earth at latitude 23.3 degrees north and longitude 86.9 degrees east.Over 126 people died, and 86 are still missing.



Bihar Drought


The condition is critical in South Bihar, where less than 50% sowing has been recorded. With Bihar receiving 45 per cent less than normal rain this year, several districts of the state are in the grip of a severe drought, even though flood has made lives miserable in the neighbouring states.



Uttrakhand Cloudburst


A cloudburst triggered floods in Uttarakhand's Uttarkashi district on Sunday, prompting the state government to dispatch teams of Indo-Tibetan Border Police (ITBP), National Disaster Response Force (NDRF) and State Disaster Response Force (SDRF) to carry out rescue and evacuation operations.
